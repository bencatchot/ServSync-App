# ServSync Marketing Product Inventory

Source-of-truth brief for marketing planning, brochures, social ads, landing-page copy, and video scripts.

This document describes what ServSync can honestly say during the controlled private beta. It separates live/beta/manual capabilities from future roadmap items so marketing work stays useful without overpromising.

## Product Overview

ServSync is a homeowner-contractor service workflow app for keeping home service work organized from first request through estimate, job/report, invoice, Home History, and follow-up reminders.

ServSync is for:

- Solo contractors and very small service teams that need a practical way to manage customer requests, estimates, jobs, reports, invoices, calendar context, and customer/home records.
- Homeowners who want a clearer way to request service, review estimates and invoices, keep service records, and remember manual follow-ups.

Current status:

- ServSync is ready for a small controlled private beta.
- The first beta should stay limited to 2 to 3 trusted contractors and friendly homeowner testers.
- ServSync is not ready for broad public launch, paid contractor launch, or public marketplace lead-generation promises.

Primary contractor value:

- Look more organized and professional from request to invoice.
- Keep work tied to the right customer, home, estimate, job/report, and invoice.
- Reduce scattered notes, manual estimate tracking, and unclear next steps.

Primary homeowner value:

- Request service with clearer context.
- Review estimates and invoices in one place.
- Keep completed work and filed invoice/service records in Home History.
- Create manual follow-up reminders without relying on memory or scattered notes.

## Current Live/Beta Workflows

### Homeowner Request

Status: Beta

Homeowners can create service requests tied to a home/property and route work to connected contractors. The beta value is clarity: requests give contractors a better starting point than scattered text messages.

Do not promise emergency dispatch, guaranteed response times, or automatic contractor matching.

### Contractor Estimate

Status: Beta

Contractors can create and send estimates with line items and context from the customer/request workflow. This is one of the main beta workflows to test.

Do not promise QuickBooks sync, automatic pricing intelligence, or legal review of estimate terms.

### Homeowner Approval

Status: Beta

Homeowners can review and accept/decline estimates. Accepted-estimate copy should stay clear that the contractor's next step is to create, schedule, or complete the job/work.

Do not imply estimate acceptance automatically schedules work or collects payment.

### Contractor Job / Checklist Report

Status: Beta

Contractors can create jobs from accepted estimates and use service-job or checklist/report workflows. Internal database naming may still use inspection terminology, but user-facing language should favor jobs, service work, and checklist/report jobs where appropriate.

Do not market this as enterprise dispatch, advanced field service management, certified inspection software, or a replacement for legally required inspection reports.

### Invoice

Status: Beta / Manual payment tracking

Contractors can create/send invoices where connected homeowner data supports it, and homeowners can view invoices. Payment collection remains outside ServSync.

Do not promise Stripe, in-app payment processing, automatic payment reminders, or accounting sync.

### Home History

Status: Beta

Homeowners can keep completed work, filed reports, invoice/service records, notes, warranty/follow-up context, and reminders in Home History. Invoice-to-Home-History filing v1 is shipped.

Do not call this "Maintenance log" in homeowner marketing. Do not imply duplicate PDF storage or automatic filing of every service record.

### Manual Home Reminder

Status: Manual

Homeowners can create, complete, and dismiss manual Home Reminders. They are useful for follow-up tasks after service work or Home History entries.

Do not promise email, text, push, recurring reminders, scheduler automation, or calendar sync.

### Calendar Visibility

Status: Beta

Homeowners and contractors can see calendar/appointment context where existing data supports it. Homeowner calendar events open read-only detail modals; contractor calendar supports event/job context.

Do not promise full external calendar sync, advanced scheduling automation, or recurring-event editing depth.

### Support / Feedback

Status: Beta

Users can submit support/feedback, and beta operations should triage feedback daily in the first week.

Do not promise 24/7 support, enterprise SLAs, or instant fixes for feature requests.

## Not Ready / Do Not Market As Live

These items should stay out of "live feature" marketing until implemented, tested, and approved:

- Payments/Stripe checkout.
- QuickBooks/accounting sync.
- Push notifications.
- Email/text reminder automation.
- Recurring reminders or scheduler automation.
- Native mobile apps.
- Full external calendar sync.
- Broad Discover/feed marketplace behavior.
- Public self-serve marketplace launch.
- Advanced analytics/admin reporting.
- Enterprise dispatch, route optimization, or workforce management.
- Automated contractor call tracking.
- Production authenticated smoke accounts.
- Public lead-volume claims.

## Target User Profiles

### First Beta Contractor Profile

Best fit:

- Solo contractor or very small team.
- Already comfortable giving direct feedback.
- Has simple service request, estimate, job/report, and invoice workflows.
- Does not require Stripe, QuickBooks, native mobile apps, SMS/email automation, or full calendar sync to test value.
- Works with friendly homeowners who can test request, estimate, invoice, Home History, and reminder flows.

Examples:

- Handyman or home repair contractor.
- Pest, lawn, pool, cleaning, HVAC, plumbing, electrical, drywall, roofing/exterior, or similar local service provider with a straightforward workflow.

### First Homeowner Tester Profile

Best fit:

- Friendly homeowner connected to a trusted beta contractor.
- Willing to test one real or realistic service workflow.
- Comfortable sending feedback and screenshots when something is confusing.
- Does not expect a polished public-marketplace experience yet.

### Users Not Ready For Beta Yet

Do not invite yet:

- High-volume field teams needing dispatch, complex roles, route optimization, or advanced admin tools.
- Contractors requiring Stripe, QuickBooks, SMS/email reminder automation, native mobile apps, or full calendar sync.
- Contractors expecting immediate public marketplace leads.
- Homeowners looking for broad self-serve contractor marketplace behavior.
- Users who are not comfortable with hands-on beta support and fast iteration.

- It should feel approachable for solo operators and very small teams.

- "Built for solo contractors and small service teams."
- "Professional workflow without enterprise complexity."
